The Lawsuit at Riot Games

This section will talk more about the recent controversy surrounding Riot Games.

In 2018, the popular online news site Kotaku, interviewed 28 current and former employees of Riot Games. The interview concerned alleged gender discrimination at the game's headquarters. Those being interviewed spoke of everything from being payed less, all the way to sexual harrassment. Then, in 2019, five current and former employees of Riot Games brought lawsuits against the company. Riot responded by filing motions to force two of these employees into private arbitrations. For context, in a private arbitration, there is no judge or jury, which makes it significantly harder for a company to be held accountable for their actions. In response to this, over 150 women from the company walked out in protest recently. They promised that they would resort to other means if Riot chose to do nothing.
